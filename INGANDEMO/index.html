Abstract


    Voice Conversion(VC) has received a lot of attention and research, especially in zero-shot many-to-many voice conversion. Recently, AdaIN-VC has implemented one-hot conversion by a variational autoencoder framework with content and timbre encoders, but the framework does not convert well in the face of unseen speakers. In this paper, we use instance normalization to complete the separation of timbre and content and introduce a generative adversarial network to synthesize the new speech. We use speaker embedding as the input of timbre to achieve zero-shot voice conversion. We also use a multi-discriminator model to train adversarially on timbre and reconstructed samples separately to enhance the conversion quality. Our experiments show that our framework has very satisfying results on unseen speakers.
